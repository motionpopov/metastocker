How to Write Keywords for Stock Photos in 2025

Learn the best practices for keywording stock photos to maximize your reach and sales on Adobe Stock, Shutterstock, and Envato.

The algorithms for major microstocks have fundamentally shifted. Gone are the days when keyword stuffing yielded higher visibility. In 2025, search engines prioritize precision, semantic meaning, and specific relevance over sheer volume.

1. The Power of the First 10 Keywords

AI search engines like Adobe Stock's focus heavily on the first ordered keywords. The traditional approach of ordering alphabetically or randomly is detrimental to your conversions.

  • Be Specific: Start with the core subject (e.g., "golden retriever puppy" instead of just "dog").
  • Use Context: Describe the action or environment (e.g., "playing in autumn park").
  • Conceptual Tagging: Include feelings or concepts (e.g., "joy," "friendship").

2. Semantic Phrasing Over Single Words

Instead of breaking a phrase like "solar eclipse" into two single tags "solar" and "eclipse", use the natural phrase.

"Semantic matching means the system understands the context of phrases better than the sum of isolated words."

Avoid the Fluff

Limit subjective adjectives. If a photo is a business meeting, tags like beautiful or amazing detract from technical accuracy like corporate boardroom or professional collaboration.

3. Leveraging AI for Automation

Manually tagging hundreds of photos takes too long. Tools like MetaStocker analyze the visual elements of your images using LLMs (Large Language Models) like GPT-5-nano to produce accurate, highly relevant metadata instantly, tailored for different platforms.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many keywords should I use today?

Aim for 25-30 highly relevant tags. Maximizing to 50 often dilutes search accuracy on modern platforms.

Should I copy keywords from top sellers?

You can draw inspiration, but blind copying without analyzing visual relevance often misfires as the algorithm flags irrelevant broad tags.
